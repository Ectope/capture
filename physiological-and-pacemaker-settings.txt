PHYSIOLOGICAL AND PACEMAKER SETTINGS
CAPTURE - Cardiac And Pacing Training Using Realistic Emulation
========================================================================


IS IT SIMULATING, OR JUST DRAWING PICTURES?
------------------------------------------------------------------------

It is a genuine simulation, not a series of pictures.

The core is a real-time, event-driven state machine that advances in
small (~10 ms) steps and runs at normal (1x) real time. On each step it,
in order:

  1. fires any conducted ventricular beat that is due (only if the AV
     node and ventricle have recovered);
  2. advances the patient's own atrial pacemaker and, where relevant,
     the ventricular escape focus;
  3. runs the pacemaker's timing cycles for the selected mode, gated by
     the refractory and blanking windows and by what the device is
     currently allowed to sense.

Every depolarisation - whether it arises from the patient's own
conduction system or from a captured pacing stimulus - is produced as a
discrete cardiac event. The ECG and arterial pressure traces are then
drawn from those events. In other words, the timing, capture, sensing,
conduction and inhibition are all genuinely computed; only the shapes of
the individual waveforms are stylised templates. Adjusting the rate,
output, sensitivity, a lead connection or the underlying rhythm causes
the behaviour to re-derive live - nothing is a pre-recorded loop. The
resulting behaviour can be checked automatically against the stream of
cardiac events, which is only possible because the rhythm is genuinely
simulated.

What it is NOT: a cellular / ion-channel biophysical model. It simulates
at the level of cardiac timing and events - which is the level
at which pacemaker behaviour (demand pacing, atrial tracking, capture,
fusion, refractory blanking) actually operates, and the level that
matters for teaching.


1. UNDERLYING RHYTHM PARAMETERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rhythm            Atrial   Ventricular          AV          PR       Conduction
                  rate     rate                 conduction  interval ratio
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sinus 40           40      40 (conducted)       yes         160 ms   1:1
Sinus 80           80      80 (conducted)       yes         160 ms   1:1
Sinus 120         120     120 (conducted)       yes         160 ms   1:1
1st-degree HB      55      55 (conducted)       yes         320 ms   1:1
2nd-degree 2:1     70      35 (every 2nd P)     yes         200 ms   2:1
2nd-degree 3:1     75      25 (every 3rd P)     yes         200 ms   3:1
Complete HB        75      30 (escape rhythm)   no          -        -
Asystole            0       0 (no escape;       yes         160 ms   1:1
                              conduction intact)

Note: for the conducting rhythms the ventricular rate is an emergent
result (atrial rate divided by the conduction ratio, after the PR
delay), not an independent driver - the value shown is what that works
out to. A ventricular escape focus only appears when AV conduction is
absent (complete heart block). Asystole is modelled as sinus arrest with
an intact conduction system.


2. DEVICE TIMING AND DETECTION SETTINGS (fixed; shared by all modes)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Setting                              Value     Role
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Paced AV interval (PAV)              200 ms    atrial-pace to ventricular-pace delay
Sensed AV interval (SAV)            180 ms    atrial-sense to ventricular-pace delay
                                               (shorter than PAV; both longer than the
                                               native PR so intact conduction inhibits)
Post-ventricular atrial refractory  300 ms    blocks tracking of retrograde / far-field P
Atrial refractory period            250 ms    after an atrial event
Ventricular refractory period       250 ms    prevents T-wave oversensing
Atrial blanking                     100 ms    sensing off briefly after an atrial event
Ventricular blanking (post-vent)    120 ms    sensing off briefly after a ventricular event
Ventricular blanking (post-A-pace)   20 ms    crosstalk guard after an atrial pace
AV-node refractory (conduction)     300 ms    a native P this soon after a ventricular
                                               event will not conduct (retrograde
                                               concealment) - no beat in the refractory
                                               period after a paced beat
Atrial myocardial refractory        200 ms    a pace landing this soon after a
                                               depolarisation cannot capture (pseudofusion)
Ventricular myocardial refractory   250 ms    as above, for the ventricle
Atrial capture threshold              2 mA    atrial output must reach this to capture
Ventricular capture threshold         3 mA    ventricular output must reach this to capture
Atrial signal amplitude               3 mV    atrial sensitivity number must be <= this to sense
Ventricular signal amplitude          8 mV    ventricular sensitivity number must be <= this to sense

A NOTE ON THE VALUES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

These values are teaching-calibrated approximations, not figures
verified against a specific device's technical manual. They are
internally consistent and reproduce the correct qualitative behaviour,
but several of them - particularly the exact refractory and blanking
durations and the power-up defaults - should be confirmed against the
manual of the specific pacemaker before being used for formal
assessment. The refractory and blanking figures in particular are
plausible values rather than device-verified numbers.
